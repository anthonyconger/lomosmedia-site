Value Analysis — August 2025

Lennar (LEN) — Value Breakdown

A scale homebuilder where the true moat is land + entitlements, not lumber and nails.

What They Do

Builds single-family communities at scale, increasingly with build-to-rent pipelines sold to institutional buyers to recycle capital faster.

Key Numbers

  • P/E in the low double-digits; modest but steady dividend.
  • Strong cash generation; active buybacks reducing share count.
  • Large controlled land bank (owned + optioned).

Moat & Disruption Risk

  • Moat: Land access, entitlement muscle, vendor scale, bulk exits.
  • Disruption: Modular/manufactured entrants + zoning shifts could compress margins.

Management & Capital Allocation

Prioritizes turnover velocity, disciplined land spend, and opportunistic buybacks.

Valuation Snapshot

Priced like a cyclical; earnings power supported by structural housing shortage. Multiple may stay grounded—returns come from execution and capital returns.

Risks That Actually Matter

  • Interest rates & credit cycles.
  • Land costs and entitlement bottlenecks.
  • Quality/litigation spikes.
  • Macro housing demand shocks.

ROI (Conservative)

~6–7% / yr

  • Assumes steady EPS, ongoing buybacks/dividend.
  • Flat multiple valuation.

Not investment advice. Educational use only.

Bottom Line

If performance stays steady, Lennar is a compounding machine—unflashy returns driven by execution and capital recycling.
